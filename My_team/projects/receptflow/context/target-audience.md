# Receptflow Target Audience Personas

## Persona 1: Sarah - Overwhelmed Practice Manager

**Profile:**
- Age: 35-45
- Role: Practice Manager or Office Manager
- Industries: Dental, medical, legal practices
- Team size: 3-10 employees
- Location: Australia-wide

**Current Situation:**
- Manages phones, scheduling, patient communication
- Fielding 50-100+ calls per day during business hours
- Missing 30-35% of incoming calls (lunch breaks, busy periods, taking other calls)
- No formal after-hours coverage
- Burning out from constant phone interruptions

**Pain Points:**
- **Lost Revenue**: Each missed call = ~$8,000 lifetime patient value (new patient acquisition)
- **Stress & Burnout**: Phone ringing constantly interrupts real work
- **Scheduling Conflicts**: Can't answer and can't properly schedule simultaneously
- **Cost Pressure**: Can't afford a full-time receptionist ($50-70k+ annually) but needs one
- **Staff Turnover**: Receptionists often leave; onboarding takes weeks
- **Inconsistent Service**: Quality depends on who answers the phone

**Goals:**
- Capture every incoming call—no more missed opportunities
- Reduce phone stress for the team
- Improve appointment booking accuracy
- Lower labor costs while maintaining service
- Free up time for higher-value work (patient care, administration)

**Buying Triggers:**
- Realizing a missed call costs thousands in lost revenue
- Staff member gone/on leave with no backup
- Frustrated patients calling back after missed calls
- Comparing to competitors who "always answer"
- Looking at hire costs vs. AI solution costs
- Wanting to extend service to after-hours/weekends

**Success Metrics:**
- Call answer rate increases to 95%+
- Time spent on phone drops by 50%
- Appointment no-shows decrease
- Increased patient satisfaction from being reached

## Persona 2: Tom - Solo Tradie

**Profile:**
- Age: 28-50
- Role: Owner/Operator
- Trades: Plumbing, electrical, painting, HVAC, carpentry
- Team size: 1-3 employees (often just himself)
- Location: Regional or metro Australia

**Current Situation:**
- On job sites most of the day (no desk)
- Gets 20-40 calls per day
- Missing 60-80% of calls while on jobs (can't answer safely/practically)
- No receptionist—can't afford one or don't want to manage one
- Some customers text, some call; inconsistent tracking
- After-hours calls during emergency work: missed opportunities

**Pain Points:**
- **Lost Callouts**: Missing calls = missing $300-800 emergency jobs
- **No System**: Notes scattered across phone, paper, memory
- **Complexity Aversion**: Can't handle complex software—needs dead simple
- **Mobile-Heavy**: Works on-site; needs solution that works on phone
- **Reputation Risk**: Customers frustrated by missed calls or slow callbacks
- **Solo Burden**: All business functions fall on one person

**Goals:**
- Capture every incoming call
- Simple setup and daily operation (minimal training)
- Know which calls are urgent/emergency (pay more attention)
- Qualify leads before calling back (save time on tire-kickers)
- Streamline booking emergency jobs
- Have peace of mind knowing calls aren't being missed

**Buying Triggers:**
- Missing a big emergency job (lost $500+ callout)
- Customer complaints about unreachability
- Realizing he's losing work to competitors with better phone systems
- A mate recommends an AI receptionist
- Realizes he can't scale without help answering phones
- Wants to take more time off without losing jobs

**Success Metrics:**
- Capture rate goes from 20% to 90%+
- Time responding to calls/messages drops significantly
- More bookings through better lead capture
- Less stress about missing calls

## Persona 3: Jessica - Multi-Location Clinic Owner

**Profile:**
- Age: 40-55
- Role: Owner/Director
- Industries: Dental clinics, physiotherapy, beauty, wellness, veterinary
- Team size: 15-50 employees across 2-5 locations
- Location: Australia (mostly metro areas with expansion plans)

**Current Situation:**
- Multiple locations with different phone systems/staff
- Each location has 1-3 receptionists
- Call volume varies by location; peak times overwhelm staff
- High receptionist turnover (burnout, inconsistent quality)
- Inconsistent experience across locations (embarrassing)
- Expansion blocked by need for more reception staff
- Some locations need multilingual support (Chinese, Vietnamese, etc.)
- Training new staff takes weeks; quality varies

**Pain Points:**
- **Staff Cost & Turnover**: $50-70k per receptionist × multiple locations = $150-350k+ yearly
- **Inconsistent Brand**: Different experience at each location
- **Scaling Blocked**: Can't expand without hiring more reception staff
- **Quality Variance**: Patient experience depends on who answers
- **Multilingual Gaps**: Can't serve non-English speaking communities effectively
- **Peak Time Chaos**: Lunch hours or after-school rush overwhelms existing staff
- **Training Burden**: Onboarding receptionists is expensive and slow

**Goals:**
- Unified phone system across all locations
- Consistent, professional caller experience everywhere
- Reduce reception labor costs significantly
- Support multilingual calls (at least English/Mandarin)
- Scale to new locations without proportional staff increases
- Free up current receptionists for patient-facing work

**Buying Triggers:**
- Receptionist quits; looking at hiring cycle
- Opening a new location and facing reception staffing costs
- Expansion plan blocked by labor availability
- Patient complaint about poor phone experience
- Realizing centralized phone system could save $100k+ yearly
- Competitor expanding faster because they can handle calls better

**Success Metrics:**
- Unified phone experience across all locations
- Receptionist headcount reduced by 40-50%
- Answer rate improves to 95%+
- New locations operational faster (less staff overhead)
- Training time drops significantly

## Persona 4: Rachel - Growth-Focused Real Estate Agent

**Profile:**
- Age: 30-45
- Role: Agent or small team leader
- Market: Australia (residential real estate)
- Team size: Solo to 3-5 agents
- Location: Metro areas (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth)

**Current Situation:**
- Listing properties, showing homes, negotiating deals
- 50-150 calls per day during business hours
- Answers most calls personally (reluctant to delegate)
- Biggest challenge: after-hours and weekend calls when she's busy or unavailable
- Not using any AI receptionist currently
- Missing high-intent after-hours buyer calls
- Competing with agents who are more reachable

**Pain Points:**
- **Lost Leads**: 85% of buyers won't call back if you miss their first call
- **After-Hours Calls**: Highest-intent calls happen 6pm-10pm and weekends (she's with families/busy)
- **Mobile Juggling**: Can't simultaneously show a property and answer phones
- **Competition**: Agents who are "always available" capture more deals
- **Qualification Time**: Spending time on tire-kickers, not serious buyers
- **Conversion Anxiety**: Wondering how many leads she's missing

**Goals:**
- Never miss a high-intent buyer call (especially after-hours)
- Qualify leads before calling back (know who's serious)
- Stay in touch with prospects faster than competitors
- Capture after-hours/weekend calls professionally
- Scale team without losing lead capture
- Know which leads are hot, warm, cold to prioritize callbacks

**Buying Triggers:**
- Lost listing because buyer reached competitor first
- Missed after-hours call from serious buyer
- Realizing competitor is available 24/7
- Looking at hiring an assistant/receptionist (cost/management burden)
- Expansion blocked by inability to handle more calls
- Wants to focus on selling, not phone-answering

**Success Metrics:**
- Capture rate for after-hours calls increases to 90%+
- Time to first contact with leads drops significantly
- More listings from being more reachable
- Closing rate improves (fewer missed prospects)
- Ability to scale team without losing call coverage

## Cross-Persona Insights

### Universal Pain Points:
1. **Missed calls = lost money** (specific dollar amounts vary by industry)
2. **No system or bad system** for managing inbound communication
3. **Burnout or cost** if relying on humans alone
4. **Can't scale** without proportional staffing increases
5. **Inconsistent quality** across team or locations

### Universal Goals:
1. Capture every call (or 95%+)
2. Know which leads are valuable (hot/warm/cold)
